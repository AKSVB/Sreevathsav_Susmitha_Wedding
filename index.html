The Cosmic Binary

In a standard planetary system, one star remains stationary while the other orbits it. But in a true binary star system—like Arundhati and Vashistha (Mizar and Alcor)—both stars orbit a shared center of mass, moving together as equals.

A Perfect Equilibrium
Just as these stars navigate the cosmos bound by a shared center of gravity, Vedic tradition holds them as the ultimate symbol of an ideal marriage: an equal partnership of shared light, mutual respect, and infinite harmony. ✨
